About IEEE WIE SBCEK

IEEE Women in Engineering (WIE) SBCEK at College of Engineering Karunagappally is part of a global network of IEEE members and volunteers dedicated to promoting women engineers and scientists and inspiring girls around the world to follow their academic interests in engineering and science.

Mission & What IEEE WIE Strives To Do

Mission

To connect, support, and inspire women and girls worldwide, and facilitate their recruitment and retention in STEM fields, fostering technological innovation and excellence for the benefit of humanity.

IEEE WIE strives to:

  • Assist with forming new IEEE WIE Affinity Groups and support ongoing activities.
  • Recognize the outstanding achievements of its members in electrical and electronics engineering through IEEE Awards nominations.
  • Organize workshops at major technical conferences to enhance networking and promote membership in IEEE WIE.
  • Advocate leadership roles for its members in IEEE governance and career advancement in their professions.
  • Promote member grade advancement to the IEEE membership grades of Senior Member and Fellow.
  • Facilitate the development of programs and activities that promote the entry into, and retention of women in engineering programs.
  • Administer the IEEE Student–Teacher and Research Engineer/Scientist (STAR) Program to mentor young women in junior high and high schools.

Governance & Structure

Governance

The IEEE WIE Committee (WIEC) is a committee of the IEEE Member and Geographic Activities (MGA) Board, reporting through the MGA Member Engagement and Life Cycle Committee (MELCC).

IEEE SBCEK — College of Engineering Karunagappally

IEEE SBCEK (Student Branch, College of Engineering Karunagappally) is an active part of IEEE Kerala Section. The branch promotes technical innovation, professional development, and community service through diverse activities, workshops, and events.

  • Encouraging students to engage in research and innovation.
  • Hosting technical workshops, hackathons, and competitions.
  • Providing networking opportunities with professionals and alumni.
  • Supporting initiatives that foster leadership and teamwork.

History & IEEE WIE Day

1993–1994

An internal Committee on Women in Engineering was formed (1993) and evolved into the formal WIE program by 1994.

29 June 1997

IEEE Women in Engineering officially established.

By 2022

Expanded to 35,000+ members and 1,000+ Affinity Groups globally.

2014

First IEEE WIE International Leadership Conference (ILC) launched to empower mid- to senior-level women in tech.

2023

Inaugural IEEE WIE Day launched (23 June–31 July) to encourage community-wide celebrations.

2025

WIE Day celebrated from 23 June through 7 July, promoting global outreach and inclusion.

Key Programs & Impact

  • Mission & Vision: Supporting women and girls in STEM globally; fostering recruitment, retention, and leadership.
  • STAR Program: Inspiring young girls through mentorship and outreach.
  • Global Tech Marathons: Empowering student members with technical skills.
  • Distinguished Lecturers: Bringing current research and industry trends to WIE members.
  • International Leadership Conference (ILC): Fostering professional development among senior women technologists.
